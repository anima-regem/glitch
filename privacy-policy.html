Privacy Policy

Glitch - Local-first Focus Tracker

Effective date: February 26, 2026

1. Overview

Glitch is designed as a local-first productivity app. Your tasks, habits, project data, and completion history are stored on your device by default.

This page explains what data Glitch handles, how permissions are used, and what choices you have.

2. Data We Process

Glitch processes the data you create inside the app, including:

  • Tasks, chores, habits, projects, milestones, and completion history.
  • App preferences such as reminders, theme, and accessibility settings.
  • Backup vault configuration details you explicitly provide.

Glitch does not require account registration to use core features.

3. Data Storage and Sharing

  • Primary app data is stored locally on your device.
  • Glitch does not operate a cloud account backend for syncing your data.
  • Data is not sold or shared for advertising purposes.

4. Backups and Encryption

If you enable backup features, Glitch can export encrypted snapshots for portability and recovery.

  • Backup exports are passphrase-protected.
  • You are responsible for keeping your backup passphrase secure.
  • Losing the passphrase may prevent restore access to encrypted backups.

5. Permissions

Depending on features you use, Glitch may request device permissions:

  • Notifications: for optional reminders you enable.
  • Microphone: for optional voice typing features you trigger.
  • Storage / file access: to select folders and read/write backup files you request.

You can manage permissions in your device settings at any time.

6. Voice Typing and Offline Model Beta

Voice typing is optional. On Android, Glitch can use local/system speech recognition and an optional downloadable offline voice model (beta).

  • Offline model files are stored locally in app-managed storage.
  • Model downloads occur only when you explicitly start a download from Settings.
  • During active model transfer/preparation, you can cancel or cancel-and-remove from Settings.
  • Large offline model options are guarded by device memory checks to reduce instability on low-memory devices.
  • If offline model runtime becomes unstable, Glitch can automatically return to native system speech recognition.
  • If fallback speech processing may use network services, Glitch requires explicit opt-in before use.

7. External Links

From settings, Glitch may open external pages (for example GitHub releases or support links). Those websites operate under their own privacy policies.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Your local app data remains on-device until you delete it, uninstall the app, or use reset functionality.
  • You can clear local data from within app settings.
  • Any backup files you exported remain where you stored them until you delete them.

9. Children's Privacy

Glitch is not intended to collect personal information from children. If you believe data was provided in error, remove it from the device and backups.

10. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as features evolve. Updates will be published on this page with a revised effective date.
